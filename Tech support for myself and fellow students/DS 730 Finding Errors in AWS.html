I had a different reason for arriving at the same generic "subprocess failed with code 1" that you did from the overall job log. There is a way to get a little deeper into the various mapper and reducer thread logs and find exactly what the deal is.

1. From your cluster summary, click on Steps.

2. The "subprocess failed with code 1" is found in stderr, but we want to dive into the specific jobs. Click on View Jobs.

 

 

3. From there, click on View Tasks.

 

4. Now we're getting somewhere: The mapper worked just fine but the reducer was the problem. For the one that says FAILED, click on View Attempts.

 

 

 

5. From here, check out one or more of the logs from the attempts in question. Click on stderr.

 

 

 

6. And, here we are. The reason it was failing was that I wanted to use the math.isclose() to test two floats that aren't exactly equal, but are close. It didn't like my import call.  So I took it out and did my test without it. It executed just fine after that.
